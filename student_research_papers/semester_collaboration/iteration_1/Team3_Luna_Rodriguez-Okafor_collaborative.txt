**ABSTRACT:**

This collaborative investigation examines Decolonizing Academic Knowledge through the integration of Astrobiology, Digital Humanities, Neurodivergent Studies perspectives. Our research demonstrates that Decolonizing Academic Knowledge operates simultaneously at multiple analytical scales, requiring collaborative approaches that can accommodate methodological pluralism while maintaining theoretical rigor. Through sustained dialogue between 3 researchers with distinct academic voices, we develop a multi-paradigmatic framework that reveals previously unexplored dimensions of Decolonizing Academic Knowledge. Key findings include: (1) Decolonizing Academic Knowledge functions as a site where different forms of knowledge intersect and inform each other, (2) collaborative research processes generate insights about both the research topic and the nature of interdisciplinary inquiry itself, and (3) effective approaches to Decolonizing Academic Knowledge require integration of individual, community, and systemic levels of analysis. Our methodology combines planetary-scale analysis and temporal research methods with computational methods and digital humanities approaches and accessibility-centered research and inclusive methodologies, creating a collaborative framework that honors multiple ways of knowing while working toward shared understanding. The implications extend beyond Decolonizing Academic Knowledge to broader questions about how academic knowledge is produced, validated, and applied in service of social transformation. This work contributes to both theoretical understanding of Decolonizing Academic Knowledge and methodological innovations for collaborative research across academic traditions.
